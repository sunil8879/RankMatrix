Here is a **clear, numbered breakdown of the methodology** used by the three major global rankings: **QS, ARWU, and CWUR**.

---

# 1. QS World University Rankings (Quacquarelli Symonds)

QS uses **6 main indicators**, each with a weight.

### **1. Academic Reputation (40%)**

* Based on global academic surveys
* Thousands of professors rate universities
* Measures **teaching & research reputation**

### **2. Employer Reputation (10%)**

* Survey of employers worldwide
* Measures **job-readiness of graduates**

### **3. Faculty/Student Ratio (20%)**

* Proxy for teaching quality
* Lower ratio = more personalized teaching

### **4. Citations per Faculty (20%)**

* Research impact per faculty member
* Uses Scopus database

### **5. International Faculty Ratio (5%)**

* % of foreign professors
* Measures global diversity

### **6. International Student Ratio (5%)**

* % of international students
* Measures global attractiveness

---

# 2. ARWU (Shanghai Ranking)

ARWU focuses almost entirely on **research excellence and scientific prestige**.

### **1. Alumni winning Nobel/Fields Medals (10%)**

* Measures historical academic excellence of graduates

### **2. Staff winning Nobel/Fields Medals (20%)**

* Current faculty recognition

### **3. Highly Cited Researchers (HiCi) (20%)**

* Researchers in top citation lists

### **4. Nature & Science papers (N&S) (20%)**

* Publications in top 2 journals globally

### **5. Indexed publications (PUB) (20%)**

* Total research output in major databases

### **6. Per Capita Performance (PCP) (10%)**

* Research output per faculty member

👉 **Key idea:** ARWU = PURE RESEARCH POWER ranking

---

# 3. CWUR (Center for World University Rankings)

CWUR is unique because it uses **no surveys at all**—only objective data.

### **1. Education (25%)**

* Alumni achievements
* Alumni success relative to university size

### **2. Employability (25%)**

* Alumni working in top global companies
* CEO / leadership positions

### **3. Faculty Quality (10%)**

* Faculty awards (Nobel, Fields, etc.)

### **4. Research Output (10%)**

* Number of research papers

### **5. High-Quality Publications (10%)**

* Papers in top journals

### **6. Influence (10%)**

* Citation impact of research

### **7. Citations (10%)**

* Total research citations

---

# 🔥 Simple Comparison

| Ranking  | Main Focus                            | Data Source Style                |
| -------- | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **QS**   | Reputation + Teaching + Employability | Surveys + citations              |
| **ARWU** | Elite research excellence             | Nobel, Nature/Science, citations |
| **CWUR** | Outcomes + research + employability   | Pure objective data (no surveys) |

---

# ⚡ One-line intuition

* **QS** → “What do people think of the university?”
* **ARWU** → “How world-class is its research?”
* **CWUR** → “What do graduates achieve in real life?”
